About This Program

What is Experimental Arts?

Experimental Arts is a sensory-led creative program for young children aged 4–7. Each week, children explore a different material, technique, or design concept — from colour mixing and texture making to simple construction and pattern design. Rooted in design thinking principles adapted for early childhood, this class sparks curiosity, builds fine motor skills, and gives children a joyful, mess-welcome space to express themselves through making.

Curriculum

What Happens Each Week

Week 1–2

Colour Exploration

Mix, layer, and discover colour relationships through painting and printing.

Week 3

Texture Making

Explore different textures using natural materials, fabrics, and found objects.

Week 4

Pattern & Rhythm

Design repeating patterns inspired by nature, textiles, and everyday objects.

Week 5

Construction & Building

Create 3D structures using recyclable materials and simple joining techniques.

Week 6

Collage & Composition

Arrange, layer, and compose mixed-media artworks developing spatial awareness.

Week 7–8

Design Story

Children create a personal art piece and describe their design choices at the showcase.

Learning Outcomes

What Your Child Gains

🎨

Creative Expression

Children develop confidence to express their ideas, feelings, and observations through art — building self-esteem and communication skills.

✋

Fine Motor Development

Cutting, tearing, painting, and building all develop the hand-eye coordination and fine motor control that supports early writing.

🌈

Colour & Visual Literacy

Understanding colour relationships and visual composition gives children a foundation for design thinking that benefits them through school.

🔍

Curiosity & Exploration

Every session is open-ended — children learn that there's no 'wrong' answer in creativity, building a growth mindset.

🗣️

Language & Storytelling

Describing their artwork develops vocabulary, storytelling, and the ability to explain their thinking — key skills for school readiness.

🤝

Social Skills

Working alongside other children in a creative space builds sharing, turn-taking, and the ability to appreciate different ways of doing things.

Is This Right for My Child?

Who Thrives in This Class

✅ Great fit if your child...

Loves making, painting, or getting their hands messy. Enjoys exploring new materials and textures. Is curious, active, and benefits from creative structured play.

💡 No experience needed if...

No art experience or skills needed — this class is designed for complete beginners. Mess is expected and very much welcome.
